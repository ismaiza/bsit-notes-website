Management Information Systems (MIS)

1. Introduction to MIS

A Management Information System (MIS) is a computerized system that collects, processes, stores, and provides information to support management decision-making, planning, and control in an organization.

2. Information System

An Information System is a combination of people, hardware, software, data, and procedures that work together to produce useful information.

3. Data vs Information

Data Information
Raw facts Processed data
Unorganized Organized and meaningful
Example: 100, 200 Example: Total sales = 300

4. Objectives of MIS

  • Provide accurate information
  • Support decision making
  • Improve efficiency
  • Assist planning and control
  • Enhance communication

5. Components of MIS

  • Hardware – Computers, servers, input/output devices
  • Software – Operating systems and applications
  • Data – Database and data files
  • Procedures – Rules and methods
  • People – Managers, IT staff, users

6. Types of Information Systems

  • Transaction Processing System (TPS)
  • Management Information System (MIS)
  • Decision Support System (DSS)
  • Executive Support System (ESS)
  • Office Automation System (OAS)

7. Transaction Processing System (TPS)

TPS handles day-to-day business transactions such as sales, payroll, and inventory.

8. Decision Support System (DSS)

DSS helps managers make semi-structured and unstructured decisions using models and analytical tools.

9. Executive Support System (ESS)

ESS provides summarized and graphical information to top-level management for strategic decision making.

10. Levels of Management

Level Role System Used
Top Level Strategic planning ESS
Middle Level Tactical decisions MIS, DSS
Lower Level Operational tasks TPS

11. Characteristics of MIS

  • Management-oriented
  • Integrated system
  • Supports decision making
  • Based on databases
  • Timely and accurate

12. MIS and Decision Making

MIS provides relevant information that helps managers:

  • Identify problems
  • Evaluate alternatives
  • Make informed decisions

13. Advantages of MIS

  • Improves productivity
  • Better decision making
  • Efficient data management
  • Improved coordination
  • Cost reduction

14. Disadvantages of MIS

  • High implementation cost
  • Requires skilled personnel
  • Security risks
  • Dependency on technology

15. Applications of MIS

  • Banking systems
  • Hospital management
  • Educational institutions
  • Inventory management
  • Human resource management

16. MIS Security and Control

  • User authentication
  • Access control
  • Data backup
  • Encryption

17. Challenges of MIS

  • Data quality issues
  • User resistance
  • System maintenance
  • Rapid technological changes

18. Role of MIS in Organizations

MIS plays a vital role in achieving organizational goals by providing timely, relevant, and accurate information.
