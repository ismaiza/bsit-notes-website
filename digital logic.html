Digital Logic Design

1. Introduction

Digital Logic Design is the study of digital systems that use binary values (0 and 1). It forms the foundation of computer hardware and digital electronics.

2. Analog vs Digital Signals

Analog Digital
Continuous values Discrete values (0 and 1)
More noise sensitive Less noise sensitive

. Binary Number System

The binary number system is base 2 and uses only two digits: 0 and 1.

Example:

Binary: 1010
Decimal: (1×8) + (0×4) + (1×2) + (0×1) = 10

. Bits and Bytes (Memory Calculation)

  • 1 bit = 0 or 1
  • 1 Byte = 8 bits
  • 1 KB = 1024 Bytes
  • 1 MB = 1024 KB
  • 1 GB = 1024 MB

Example 1: How many bits are in 4 KB?

4 KB = 4 × 1024 × 8
= 32768 bits

Example 2: How many bits are needed to address 256 locations?

256 = 2⁸
Answer: 8 bits

. Boolean Algebra

Boolean Algebra is used to analyze and simplify digital circuits. It works with two values: 0 (False) and 1 (True).

Basic Boolean Operators

  • AND (·)
  • OR (+)
  • NOT (')

. Logic Gates

AND Gate

Output is 1 only when both inputs are 1.

ABOutput
000
010
100
111

OR Gate

Output is 1 if any input is 1.

NOT Gate

Inverts the input value.

NAND Gate

NAND is the inverse of AND gate. It is a universal gate.

NOR Gate

NOR is the inverse of OR gate. It is also a universal gate.

XOR Gate

Output is 1 when inputs are different.

. Universal Gates

  • NAND Gate
  • NOR Gate

Universal gates can be used to implement any digital circuit.

. Combinational Circuits

Combinational circuits depend only on present inputs.

  • Half Adder
  • Full Adder
  • Multiplexer
  • Decoder

. Sequential Circuits

Sequential circuits depend on present input and previous output.

  • Flip-Flops
  • Counters
  • Registers

. Applications of Digital Logic

  • Computers
  • Digital clocks
  • Mobile phones
  • Embedded systems
